French Toast Recipe

What could be better than enjoying French Toast for breakfast! I like to use thicker bread for my french toast recipe but you can still use regular white bread, the trick is to not let the thinner bread soak too long as it will make your french toast soggy. I also enjoy making french toast to freeze for a quick an easy breakfast anytime, just pop the french toast from the freezer to your plate and in the microwave for a quick hearty breakfast in the morning!

Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • about 16 slices bread, (French bread, Italian Deli, Texas Toast, white bread)
  • butter for cooking

Directions

  1. In a bowl that is large enough to dip your bread slices, whisk together the eggs, sugar, vanilla, and cinnamon. Mix in the milk last.
  2. Preheat a large skillet over medium heat or a griddle (350 degrees), melt about 1-2 tablespoons butter.
  3. While butter is melting, dip a few slices of bread (how ever many slices will fit in your skillet or griddle) in the french toast mixture for 3 seconds per side and place in the skillet or griddle and cook for 3-4 minutes per side until golden brown.
  4. If you are serving fresh french toast for breakfast you can keep them warm in the oven on a cookie sheet at 250 degrees until ready to serve.

French Toast Toppings

  • Serve warm with butter
  • Maple Syrup
  • Fresh Fruit
  • Whipped Cream Topping
  • Powdered Sugar
Great Tip!

**For Freezing**
Allow it to cool completely before storing in a freezer safe container or bag for up to 3 months. To enjoy your french toast just reheat in a skillet, toaster or microwave for a quick delicious morning breakfast!
